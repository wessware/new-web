SET KENYA NEWS

The Set Kenya Magazine Homestrech milestone


Monday March 22, 2021

The long-awaited Set Kenya magazine has successfully gone through production and we are happy to announce to you that it is almost ready. A digital final-copy of the magazine was submitted to the executive committee on Tuesday March 16, 2020 by the Wessware Technology lab for proofing and verification. Further on during the month will follow the printing of the magazine.
This huge milestone has been a collective effort of all the Set Kenya members, associates and other stakeholders of the organization. We wish to utterly thank all those who contributed by writing and submitting their articles, on time. More applaud goes to the design team for the exemplary work and to the executive committee for their efficient coordination of events since the inception of this project in July 2019.
A member-friendly cost-per-copy will be arrived upon by the executive committee later on after ascertaining the total cost of production, and this information will be made available to members and contributors who wish to make purchases of the same.
Plans for the second edition are underway and more members are urged to come forth and contribute, keep checking with us for more information on this.

SET KENYA NEWS

The First Set Kenya Sunday 2021

 Sunday March 7, 2021


On the first Set Kenya Sunday in 2021, Set Kenya on March 7, 20210 held a successful training session on the importance of mindset and a proper skill set in Mentorship. The session ran from 1430hrs to around 1700hrs. The session speaker of Eugene Okanda, a student at Kenyatta University Main Campus. He, in his talk emphasized on the importance of a proper mindset when heading out to mentor young people. He also insisted on the importance of you yourself having a mentor to whom you look up to. Further on, he insisted on the importance of prayer, faith and being born-again as essential requirements of a good and successful mentor.
Further on after the session, a prayer services was held where members present supplicated a number of prayer requests submitted by the executive committee members who were present.
Announcements were made for the forthcoming Kiambu county Mega mission and some of the plans to make it a success were laid out by the organizing secretary. Most of which included fund drives and prayer requests. Also included was information about the Set Magazine, that was to be released later on during the month.
In concluding the service there was a cake cutting session where members present shared a cake that was a sponsorship of the executive committee. Leading the session were associates Ben Malova and Stephen Shisia. Members shared a cup of Uji thereafter and departed at their own pleasure. Find the recollections here.

SET KENYA NEWS

Set Kenya first Year Induction 2021

 Saturday March 6, 2021


As a follow up of the Clubs fair held in November 2020, Set Kenya held a first-year induction session on the morning of March 6, 2021 till midday. New members were enlightened on the core values, the mission and the vison of the organization. They were educated on the importance of upholding and living by these core values at all times of their service in the organization. Also mentioned during the session was the Set Kenya constitution, which news members were urged to get acquainted with.
Also covered was how to mentor young students. The pros and cons. What to consider, and how to handle different kinds of audiences. Also, how to speak in public and how to overcome stage fright.
Present during were members of the Executive committee; the treasurer, the Organizing secretary, the secretary, the publicity secretary and the projects coordinator. Invited, was Milimo Oscar, the organizing secretary, 2018-2019 spiritual year.
Refreshments were served and members departed thereafter. Find the recollections here.
